My Words

I'm not angry... I'm just tired. Not tired of you, but tired of always trying to explain what's going on inside my heart. Every time something hurts me, I search for the right words, hoping that maybe this time you'll understand. But after a while, you start wondering if explaining even makes a difference. Sometimes I wish you could just feel it. I wish you could look at me and know that I'm not okay, even when I smile. I wish my silence could speak to you the way my words never seem to. When I become quiet, it doesn't mean I don't care. It doesn't mean I want distance. It means I'm fighting with thoughts I don't know how to put into words. I'm scared that if I tell you everything I'm feeling, I'll still end up feeling misunderstood. You know what hurts the most? It's not that we have misunderstandings. Every friendship has those. What hurts is feeling like my silence goes unnoticed, as if the moment I stop explaining, everything between us becomes quiet too. I never expected you to fix my problems. I never expected perfect words or perfect answers. I only hoped that you'd stay. That you'd sit beside me, even if we said nothing. That you'd remind me, without speaking, that I'm not facing everything alone. Sometimes people don't need advice. Sometimes they don't need solutions. Sometimes they just need one person who notices that something has changed, one person who says, "I'm here. You don't have to explain everything." Maybe this bond doesn't have a name. Maybe it doesn't fit into friendship or anything else. But whatever it is, it means more to me than you probably realize. That's why your absence feels louder than anyone else's, and your understanding means more than you could ever imagine. So if you ever find me silent again, please don't assume I've stopped caring. Don't think I've given up on us. Maybe I'm just hoping that, for once, you won't wait for me to say what's wrong. Maybe I'm hoping you'll simply stay... because sometimes the deepest feelings are the ones that never find the courage to become words. You know what hurts the most? It isn't watching her go back to someone who keeps hurting her. It's realizing that no matter how much I care, no matter how quietly I stand by her, no matter how many times I stay when everyone else leaves... I'm still never the person she understands my words... Every time she's hurt, I'm there. I listen without judging. I stay without asking for anything. I remind her that she deserves better, even when she can't see it herself. I hold together the pieces someone else keeps breaking. And then... the moment that person comes back, she goes back too. That's the moment something inside me quietly breaks. Not because she chose someone else... But because, for a second, I wonder if I was ever important enough to be seen. It makes me question everything. Was my presence only valuable when she was hurting? Was I just a safe place until the storm passed? Sometimes, the deepest pain isn't being left behind. It's realizing that no matter how much you mean to someone, they still keep choosing the person who makes them cry. I never wanted to replace anyone. I never expected to become the most important person in her life. I just hoped that one day she'd realize that the person who never gave up on her was standing beside her all along. Maybe some people are only meant to catch others when they fall... Never to be the reason they finally stop falling. And I think that's the part that makes me feel the most... invisible. Do you know what's strange? The people who hurt us the most somehow become the ones we keep running back to. And the people who quietly stay... who listen, who wait, who never let us face things alone... slowly become invisible. I don't mind standing beside you. I don't mind listening to the same story a hundred times. I don't mind being there when everything falls apart. What hurts is knowing that my place in your life only begins after someone else has already broken you. It's like I'm good enough to heal the wounds... But never enough to be the reason you don't get hurt again. Sometimes I wonder if you'll ever notice the person who's always been there without asking for attention. The one who never made promises, but kept showing up. The one who never wanted anything from you except to see you at peace. Maybe one day you'll look back and realize... The safest place was never the person who kept making you cry. It was the person who silently stayed every single time you did. I never competed with anyone. I never wanted to prove I was better. I just thought that one day, consistency would matter. That one day, staying would mean more than leaving and coming back. But I was wrong. It's a strange feeling... watching someone forgive the same person over and over, while your words go unnoticed. I don't blame you for your choices. I just wish, for once, you could see the person who's been quietly standing beside you all this time. Not asking to be chosen. Just hoping not to be forgotten. Maybe the hardest part isn’t watching you go back to the person who hurts you. Maybe the hardest part is knowing that every time you’re hurt, I become the place where you rest, the place where you heal, the place where you feel understood. And I’m grateful that you trust me that much. I really am. But some days, I wonder if you only remember me when things fall apart. Because when you’re happy with them, I become quieter in your life. I stop hearing about your day, your random thoughts, your little worries—the things that used to make me feel close to you. I don’t want to take anyone’s place, and I don’t expect you to choose me over anyone else. I just wish the bond we share mattered even when everything in your life is going right. Because being someone’s peace feels beautiful, but being someone’s temporary peace feels heartbreaking. And maybe that’s why I stay quiet sometimes—not because I’m upset, but because I’m trying to understand where I truly belong in your life. Sometimes I think the saddest thing isn't being ignored. It's being remembered only when things go wrong. But the moment you're hurt, confused, or broken... somehow, you find your way back to me. And I'll be honest—I never hated being there for you. What hurts is realizing that I seem to have a place in your bad days, but not in your good ones. I know I can't decide who matters to you. I know feelings and connections aren't something anyone can control. But there are moments when I sit quietly and wonder... If I disappeared for a while, would anything change? Would my absence be noticed the same way my presence is needed? Because sometimes I feel like a chapter you only open when life gets difficult. And maybe that's why it hurts so much. I didn't know after saying all this stuff I will be misunderstood or not... I have many more thins to say but....now I really want some time alone...
